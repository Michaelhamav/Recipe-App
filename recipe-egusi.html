OUR RECIPE

2 servings

50 minutes

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup ground melon seed
  • 2 tbspoons vegetable oil
  • 1 diced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • 1 tspoon ginger
  • 1 tbspoon salt
  • 1 pound beef or fish
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tbspoons parsley
  • Fresh pepper

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Rinse Melon and soak in water for 30min, drain & grind into fine paste.
  2. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat, add onion, garlic and ginger, saute until softened .
  3. Add the ground Egusi Paste and stir well. Add beef, chicken or fish and cook until browned, add water, salt, black pepper and fresh pepper. Stir well
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20-25 minutes or until thickens
  5. Add Parsley or any vegetable of choice and stir well. Taste and adjust seasoning
  6. Serve Hot with fufu, garri or rice.

Egusi Soup

2 servings

50 minutes

Egusi Soup is a traditional Nigerian soup originating from the Igbo and Yoruba tribes. A popular Nigerian dish made with ground melon seeds It's a staple dish in Nigerian Cuisine, often served with fufu or garri.

The soup is rich in protein, fiber and vitamins, support digestive health, good source of minerals like iron and potassium

NOTES

Grind the egusi finely to avoid texture issues, adjust the amount of water to achieve desired consistency and use blender if needed to grind melon seed.
