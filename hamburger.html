Tasty Hamburger

A staple of American cuisine
Hamburg Germany | 19th Century

  • Preparation time: 30 - 37 minutes

  • Origin: The hamburger, a staple of American cuisine, has its roots in Hamburg, Germany, where a similar dish called "Hamburg steak" was served in the 18th and 19th centuries. German immigrants brought this concept to the United States, where it evolved into the modern hamburger.

  • Nutritional Value: Calories: 350 Protein: 30g | Fat: 20g | Saturated Fat: 8g | Cholesterol: 60mg | Sodium: 400mg | Carbohydrates: 25g | Fiber: 2g | Sugar: 5g

Additional Info:

Preservation:

  • Store raw ground beef in an airtight container at 4°C (39°F) for up to 2 days.
  • Cooked burgers can be refrigerated for up to 3 days or frozen for 2 months.

    • Variations:

  • Veggie burger: substitute beef with black beans, mushrooms, or lentils.
  • Cheeseburger: add melted cheese.
  • Bacon cheeseburger: add crispy bacon.

    • Cultural Significance:

    Hamburgers symbolize American convenience food and fast-paced lifestyle. Fast food chains like McDonald's and Burger King revolutionized the industry. Also, Hamburgers represent nostalgia, comfort food, and social gatherings.
