DISH INFO

Jollof Rice + Chicken

For Healthy Life
Native West Africa | 14th Century

  • Preparation time: 1 hour 30 minutes

  • Origin: Jollof Rice originated from West Africa, specifically from the Wolof Empire (present-day Senegal and Gambia) in the 14th century.

  • Nutritional Value: Calories | Carbs |Protein

Additional Info:

Spread:

Jollof Rice spread throughout West Africa due to trade and migration, becoming a staple dish in countries like Ghana, Nigeria, Liberia, and Sierra Leone.

Variations:

Different countries have unique variations of Jollof Rice, with Ghanaian and Nigerian versions being the most popular. Ingredients and spices differ, leading to a long-standing "Jollof Wars" rivalry between the two nations.

Cultural Significance:

Jollof Rice holds significant cultural and symbolic value in West African societies, representing unity, hospitality, and community. It's often served at special occasions like weddings, festivals, and holidays.
